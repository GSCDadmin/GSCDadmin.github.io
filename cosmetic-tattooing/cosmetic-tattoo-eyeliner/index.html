Eyeliner

What is semi permanent eyeliner?

Eyeliner semi-permanent makeup is good for people that have little time for applying makeup, or have a sporting career/lifestyle or any other factor that prevents the conventional makeup from staying on. People who have had surgery can also benefit greatly from semi-permanent makeup.

Before and after semi permanent eyeliner.

Who’s it for?

Semi-permanent makeup is a cosmetic procedure recommended for those who:

  • Have dull looking eyes
  • Have oily skin
  • Enjoys sport
  • Recently had double eyelid surgery
  • Have a small gap between their eyes

What’s the procedure of semi permanent eyeliner tattooing?

Eyeliner semi-permanent makeup is good for covering flaws around your eyes and enhancing your natural beauty. Eye makeup can be very effective, but nothing is completely smudge proof. Even in the case of longer lasting products, the correct application is challenging. Semi-permanent eyeliner can be applied in varying degrees; far thinner than regular cosmetics or more boldly if a stronger impact is required.

Different techniques, such as dotting between lashes, can be effective but subtle; strong lines can be used for intensity, or to change the apparent shape of the eye.

A deep black eye line fills gaps between inner lashes which enhances volume. The thickness of a line is decided during consultation with the client.

  • Deep black eyeline - Fills gaps between inner lashes to enhance volume.
  • Thin Line – Gives you clear looking and natural eyes.
  • Thick Line – Gives you a more intense and bold look.
  • Tear Line - This operation adds a natural looking depth to your eyes by enhancing the tear line.
  • Full Under Line – Gives you a strong and bold look.
  • Middle Point Under Line - Enhances the colour underneath your pupil
  • End Point Under Line - Enhances the colour of the tail of your eyes, giving you wider looking eyes.
  • Finish Line - This operation gives you a balanced looking eye shape

Is there any downtime?

There can be a little redness on the day of the procedure, much like getting a regular tattoo. It’s important to follow strict aftercare for the best results.
