Platelet Rich Plasma (PRP)

Healing from the inside

Made famous as the vampire facelift, PRP has been taking off in all aspects of medicine. Initially used to promote extra healing after orthopaedic surgery, it’s found its way into chronic wounds, ligamentous injuries, sports medicine, and now aesthetics. The Kardashian’s swear by it, but since the treatment started further advances have been made in the preparation and delivery which we can offer today to our patients

What is it?

Platelets are the cells that our body uses to start the repair process after injury. If you cut your finger, platelets which normally live in blood vessels come in contact with skin, and start to build a wall of fibrin and collagen to stop bleeding. They then release growth factors, and get nearby stem cells to start re-building the cells and networks to get things healthy again. Essentially we use this system to trick the skin into repairing our ageing tissue.

Who’s it for?

It’s for anyone who wants to improve the integrity, elasticity and firmness of their skin. We can use it as a facial procedure using our electronic injector to deliver it all over the face, neck or décolletage area (all separate treatments), or delivery it to very specific areas such as the dark circles under the eye.

Since it’s a healing and boosting procedure, it works really well with Aqua-shine to give a moisture boost, and improve the skin quality in one session.

It also works incredibly well for acne scarring and other scar revisions, sometimes on its own or combined with other therapies.

What’s the procedure?

We take a sample of blood, and spin it to separate the red cells and plasma. We then take that plasma, add some proprietary elements to prepare the platelets, and further separate the plasma from the platelets. This achieves a higher concentration of platelets than is available using other commercial systems.

This is then injected to the exact depth of skin we need using our electronic injector, or using manual methods. Mixed with dermal fillers we achieve a volumising and skin rejuvenating effect.

Is there downtime?

If combined with Aqua-Shine there can be small visible lumps in the skin for 24-72 hours following, but each day generally improves. On its own, the lumps last less than 24 hours in general. There will be some redness, but this is reduced by using a cool compress following the procedure.
