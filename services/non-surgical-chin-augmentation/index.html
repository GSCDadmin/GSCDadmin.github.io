Non-surgical Chin Augmentation

Chin up buttercup

Now we’ve come to the pointy end of the face. What a great area to work with. Simple enough in its appearance, but essential to the structure and look of the entire face. A little well thought out modification here can not only lengthen a face and bring balance, but also make it look thin, improve a non-existent neckline and remove chin dimpling.

Slight elongation of the chin can improve the appearance of the jawline and neck.

What is it?

As with the non-surgical rhinoplasty, it’s the introduction of volume into the chin. Using either HA, PLA or CaHA, we add substance and place it in a way that looks absolutely natural.

Who’s it for?

For those who are after some balance to a short lower third of the face. A retruding, short, or squared off chin often benefits immensely from some added volume. Volume added a little higher in the chin, below a large lower lip can also give the appearance of a more balanced lower third of the face.

What’s the procedure?

It may sound simple, but we really need to take into consideration the entire face before we do anything. The direction of projection, angle of the jaw, laxity of the skin, the crease under the bottom lip – they all play an important role. Once all this is factored in, we can being to place the volume where it’s needed.

We ask people to not sleep on their face, or have such a big night out they wake with the head resting on the dining room table after this procedure.

Is there downtime?

You can continue with your daily activities immediately, placing weight on the chin. Sleeping on your face, resting your chin on your desk can all alter the shape of the results in the first 2 weeks.
