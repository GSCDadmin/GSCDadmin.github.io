Acne scarring

The troubles with our hormonal teenage years often gets left behind once we grow up and start living our normal adult lives, unless they haven’t.

Acne scarring can be a reminder of some difficult years. Although it’s a burden we shared with a lot of our peers, the scars can continue to live on. There are some amazing treatments available these days, and different types of scars require different treatments.

What is it?

Well acne scarring is the result of severe inflammation and damage to the skin from large acne cysts. Once these rupture, they can leave a defect that gets filled with hard, abnormal scar tissue. There are about six distinct types of acne scars, each of which respond different to different treatments.

Who’s it for?

For anyone with acne (or chickenpox) scarring that wants an improvement in their skin

What’s the procedure?

There’s not really one procedure for all acne scars. We can use a range of modalities for different types of scars to improve how they look. Some deep atrophic scars need dermal fillers, tethered scars need subcision. Icepick and boxcar scars do really well with a chemical peel called TCA CROSS (trichloracetic acid chemical reconstruction of skin scars). PRP, Aqua-shine, ablative and non-ablative lasers all have a role also in improving the collagen network under these scars so this is more a tailored treatment to each individual.
