Calf slimming

Sexual dimorphism refers to the differences between the genders of an animal in terms of its physical characteristics. This is a guiding principal in traditional aesthetic medicine. And while the difference between males and females in terms of facial features can vary by gender, it also varies by age, ethnicity and cultural norms.

One area that emphasises this is the size of the lower leg, or the calf muscles. While some men spend many hours at the gym trying to obtain large, defined hypertrophied calves, women can grow to dread theirs if they’ve inherited a muscular physique in the lower legs.

What is it?

Calf slimming involves injecting ToxinA into the strongest and most visible parts of the calf muscle. This weakens that section and it begins to shrink in size. This has been an incredibly popular procedure in East Asia, mainly in Korea and Japan.

Who’s it for?

For people who feel their calves are too large and would prefer a slender lengthy look in the legs. It’s quite difficult to achieve this without reducing muscle bulk, as there is not much in the way of subcutaneous fat in this area.

What’s the procedure?

Your doctor will mark an area of your medial gastrocnemius muscle which will be injected with ToxinA. This is the area we want to achieve a reduction in size. Sometimes we also inject the lateral (or outer) aspect of the muscle, although this doesn’t often cause a visible enlargement.

Over 2-4 weeks we observe the reduction in size, and can plan further injections to reduce the size further. We don’t inject maximum doses in a single session, as reducing the muscle too much may leave us feeling weak in the lower legs. Slow and steady is the safest and most appropriate way to go when treating this area.

Is there downtime?

As with other ToxinA injections, the only downtime would be from excessive brusing or swelling, neither of which are common. You can immediately carry on with your day, but we encourage you don’t perform any excessive activity such as running, weight training, or sports for the next 24 hours.
