Skin Cancer Treatment

“I don’t want wrinkles, but it’s skin cancer that I truly fear – Janine Di Giovanni”

The skin cancer doctors at GS Cosmetic Doctors and GS Health are able to provide a comprehensive skin check, give the most up to date information on skin protection, and if necessary, use the best equipment to remove anything suspicious for examination in a certified laboratory.

They hold qualifications in the assessment, diagnosis, removal and treatment through the University of Queensland. So rest assured that your skin is in safe hands.

What’s involved?

If you’ve never had a skin check, it can be different to a normal doctors visit. After we’ve had a chat about your sun exposure and family history, you can discuss any lesions that are worrying you.

We then ask that you undress to your underwear behind our privacy curtain, and use we use a sheet to examines specific areas of skin at a time, and look at suspicious lesions with a dermatoscope.

Who’s it for?

Anyone who is worried about spots on their skin, who think they have a new mole or dark spot, or an old spot is changing or growing. It’s important to know your skin type, and you can find out by doing the quiz at GS Health (link to skin cancer page or skin type tester).

What’s the procedure?

After a skin check, if there is nothing suspicious we can discuss how often you personally need a skin check. Everyone is different depending on his or her individual risk.

Suspicious lesions are removed using a punch biopsy and sent to the lab for analysis. Anything that looks like it needs to come out is removed using surgical techniques to clear the lesion of concern, and to achieve a great cosmetic outcome. For more information please visit our skin cancer information page at gshealth.com.au

Is there downtime?

A removal of a skin cancer, or a biopsy to investigate means taking away a small area of skin. This means we need to dress and care for the resulting wound left behind. This is usually dressed with appropriate bandages and reviewed frequently. You can continue with your regular activities as long as it doesn’t directly interfere with the scar. Your doctor will advise you of the appropriate aftercare.
