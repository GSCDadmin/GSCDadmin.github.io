Dermal fillers

Build volume 1mL at a time

Fillers are the ultimate sculptor’s tool in cosmetics, and can be used in a variety of ways to achieve our goals. Targeting under filled and thin lips, to hollowing of the temples, loss of volume in the cheeks, or deepening of expressive lines, fillers placed carefully and strategically in the right places can alleviate all these and cause instant beautification or rejuvenation.

Before and after filler in the lips.

What is it?

Fillers are almost as simple as they sound. They fill areas that can do with more volume. But they’re also much more.

Depending on the most appropriate product for the job, chosen carefully in consultation with you, we choose products based on their longevity, smoothness, resistance to deformity, and ability to reflect and refract light. Most commonly we use Hyaluronic Acid (HA) based fillers as we know it’s safe and has an excellent safety profile, and gives us a selection of all the characteristics we mentioned.

However there are calcium hydroxyapatite (CaHA) and poly-caprolactone (PCL) fillers which don’t just fill, but stimulate collagen. These can last a lot longer, and are suitable for specific areas and patients wanting a longer result.

Before and immediately after filler in the cheeks and nasolabial folds, you can see improvement in the tear trough also.

Who’s it for?

Again, this can be for anyone. When used to rejuvenate, we typically use it to rebuild lost volume. This is often in the mid face and cheeks, temples, and lips.

When used for transformation and enhancement, we can place it anywhere needed. Lips can become fuller or lifted, plumped or pouted, keeping it subtle of course. Cheeks with more volume placed strategically to shine light, making the face look narrower, or heightened, and it can be used to give a non-surgical rhinoplasty, or chin augmentation, to change facial proportions to what feels natural or right to you.

What’s the procedure?

This is the one time I’ll be a bit vague. It really depends on what we’re trying to achieve. We’ll sit and spend time together, planning what we want to do carefully, and especially outline what we can’t do. Then we apply a numbing cream to the areas needed, map out what we want, and inject the filler using a very thin needle, or a blunt cannula. The depth and amount has to be carefully planned depending on the outcome we want to achieve, so come in and talk to us to see what we can do together.

Is there downtime?

In short, no. As we use very fine instruments the risk of bruising and bleeding is small, although some people have a predisposition to bruise easily. Filler to the lips tend to cause some swelling which subsides in a few days, but you will be fine to go out and carry on with your day immediately.

We ask that you don’t have a facial massage, fall asleep on your face, or relax in excessive heat (sauna/steam room) for at least 24 hours after receiving your fillers.
