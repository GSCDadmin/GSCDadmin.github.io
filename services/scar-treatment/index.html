Scar treatment

The marks humans leave are too often scars

The incomplete and improper healing of a wound, scars are often reminders of a trauma that has never left us. And like the many injuries that can cause them, there are wide ranges of scars that can develop.

The first thing we must do is establish the type of scar we’re dealing with. Acne scarring for example, comes in about 6 different varieties, each having its own best form of treatment. Raised scars may be keloid, or hypertrophic, again both having a completely different underlying mechanisms, and therefore alternative treatments.

What is it?

Scar treatment is the process of reducing the size or visibility of a scar, and allowing the tissue to complete its normal healing process. In the case of ice pick or boxcar scarring typical of severe acne, a chemical micro peel is most appropriate to eat away the abnormal tissue, and allow full healing.

Regardless of the method, the desired outcome is the same, a less visible, more acceptable healed area of skin.

Who’s it for?

Anyone who wants to put their past events behind them. While scars can be worn with pride and meaning, not all scars are that important. It may just mean we were hormonally unlucky as teenagers, had a mishap in the kitchen or have an event we’d rather not be reminded of all the time.

What’s the procedure?

At GS Cosmetic Doctors the following treatments are available for improving scars, come chat to one of our doctors to see if these are suitable for your type of scar

  • TCA CROSS (icepick or boxcar scars)
  • Subcision (tethered scars)
  • Excision (large deep scars)
  • Dermal filling (atrophic acne or chickenpox scars)
  • PRP (extensive acne scarring)
  • Serial corticosteroid injections (keloid scars)
  • ToxinA injections (hypertrophied scars)

Is there downtime?

Again this depends on the procedure performed. Generally with acne treatment, other than PRP, Aqua-shine and dermal fillers, there is some downtime. Subcision and punch biopsies need to heal and need dressings. TCA cross will leave little white dots that disappear in 24 hours, but darker skin types will have a dark spot in its place. This will take time to resolve, but also results in improved skin tone and quality.
