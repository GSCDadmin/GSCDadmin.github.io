Aqua-shine

“My ability to turn good news into anxiety is rivalled only by my ability to turn anxiety into chin acne. –Tina Fey”

Although doctors that deal with aesthetic medicine often see acne scarring and fight to repair it, those in general practice see it at the earliest stages. It’s rare that people see it all the way through to gain an understanding of the process from beginning to end. With our experience In both cosmetic medicine as well as general practice we can help our acne patients minimize the active early acne, to help prevent it develop into permanent scarring.

What is it?

Acne is essentially the inflammation and blockage of our pilosebaceous glands, or the hair follicles. This is caused by dead skin, excessive oil, and inflammation. The bacteria Propionibacterium Acne is usually involved, and a combination of all the above with hormones and our genetic predisposition can make it quite a severe problem.

Who’s it for?

Anyone with acne, whether it’s mild and intermittent, or extensive and ongoing, can benefit from a review and advise about minimising its effects.

What’s the treatment?

If only there were a single treatment or miracle cure. Late night TV ads have turned the treatment of acne into a cash cow, but it can be a lot more straightforward than it’s made out to be. It doesn’t mean it’s easy however.

It’s about handling the main causation of acne, and choosing the most appropriate treatment for you. And while medications can be helpful, using adjunctive cosmeceutical treatments can help with the dryness and irritation of acne too to make the treatment work quicker, and keep the skin feeling healthy. Pop in to any of our doctors either at GS Cosmetic Doctors or GS Health for an assessment and treatment plan.
