Skin brightening

A chronic condition characterised by symmetrical, blotchy, brownish facial pigmentation. It’s much more common in women and can be difficult to treat. It’s become very popular in Asia to undergo laser therapy to try and reduce the signs of melasma, however most laser dermatologists advise that laser is only an added treatment, not the mainstay.

What’s the treatment and who’s it for?

Since melasma is multifactorial, the treatment is customised for you. Before you meet your doctor there are a few things you can do to reduce your melasma and stop it from progressing.

  • Use broad spectrum, very high protection (SPF 50+) sunscreen applied to the whole face every day. If this is difficult, then layering a foundation, and moisturiser which both contain sunscreen can be helpful
  • Protect your face from direct sunlight, wear a broad brimmed hat, use the sun visor of your car regularly
  • Use a mild cleanser, and a light moisturiser

A combination of topical therapy, and possibly oral medication will be used to reduce the visibility of melasma. This can take several months, and its important to know that vigilance is required to keep it at bay.
