Anti-wrinkle injections

Freeze lines, reverse time

When people think of cosmetic medicine, they think of botulinum toxin A (ToxinA for short). It spawned an industry quite accidentally when an eye surgeon used it to stop an upper eyelid flickering. It was noted that it raised the eyebrow and reduced crow’s feet, and we haven’t looked back since.

People who haven’t experienced what anti-wrinkle injections can do are often afraid they’ll lose all expression, or look very un-natural. But we’ve moved away from the era of freezing the face and the plastic look.

What is it?

ToxinA works to selectively block the connection between nerves and muscles. Now this may sound scary, but in expert hands there’s nothing to fear. There are three major brands available in Australia, and due to TGA advertising regulation we’re unable to name them. We’ll refer to them as X, D and B, and at GS Cosmetic Doctors we have all of them available. We choose the most appropriate product carefully depending on the area we’re treating.

Who’s it for?

o While it’s commonly thought of as being used to erase wrinkles (and it certainly does) it’s more commonly used these days to prevent the formation of deep wrinkles. When you raise your eyebrows, or furrow them in the sun, you might notice lines appear which fade when you relax. With repetitive frowning these can become permanent or static. People often prefer to prevent them occurring in the first place, giving the everlasting youthful look. However, it reaches far beyond that and can be used for the following;

  • Facial Slimming
  • Excessive sweating in the armpits or palms
  • Calf reduction
  • Neckline slimming
  • Chin dimpling
  • Gummy smile
  • Brow lift
  • Mouth lift
  • Crow’s feet
  • Hypertrophic scar revision

What’s the procedure?

During the consultation with your doctor, we assess how wrinkles and lines form on your face individually. Then we can plan the areas that need toxin, and how many units to place in each. These are injected using extremely fine needles either into the muscle directly, or just under the skin above the muscle. The effect starts from 2-3 days depending on the product, and lasts 3-6 months depending on the dose, product and area treated.

Is there downtime?

While there may be small bumps where they were placed under the skin, these resolve by the end of the day. There is a small risk of bruising, but these are greatly minimized by correct needle placement and use of compression and ice. Other than that, you really can get this at lunch and continue on with your day.
